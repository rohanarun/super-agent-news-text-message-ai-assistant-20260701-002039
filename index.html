Why text-message assistants are taking off

SMS-based AI assistants are resurging because they remove app friction and meet users where they already are. Recent launches like TXTDrop’s SMS assistant and Poke’s text-driven agents show growing demand for messaging-first AI ([theaijournal.com](https://aijournal.com), [techcrunch.com](https://techcrunch.com)). Major platforms are responding too — Apple’s upgraded Siri and Google’s more agentic Gemini highlight how personal assistants are becoming more proactive ([apple.com](https://apple.com), [blog.google](https://blog.google)).
